The life cycle of a butterfly is a fascinating process that consists of four stages: egg, caterpillar (larva), pupa (chrysalis), and adult butterfly.

1. Egg: The female butterfly lays her eggs on a leaf or twig, usually near a food source for the caterpillars.

2. Caterpillar: Once the egg hatches, a small caterpillar (also known as a larva) emerges. It begins feeding on leaves and growing rapidly, shedding its skin several times as it grows.
