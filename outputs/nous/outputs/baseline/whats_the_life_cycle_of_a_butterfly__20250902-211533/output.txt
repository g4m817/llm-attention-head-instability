The life cycle of a butterfly, officially known as metamorphosis, consists of four stages, which are egg, caterpillar (larva), pupa, and butterfly. Each stage has a unique set of physical characteristics and behaviors associated with it.

In the egg stage, the female butterfly lays her fertilized eggs on a host plant from which she has collected pollen. These eggs are typically small, yellow, and pearly, and each one hatches into a caterpillar within a few days.
