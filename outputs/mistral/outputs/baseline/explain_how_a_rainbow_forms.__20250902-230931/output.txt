In the grandeur of our sky, a beautiful spectacle unfolds, often following a rain shower, often after a tear tells its tale. This awe-inspiring phenomenon, not to be confused with the forbidden term, is known as a rainbow.

The formation of a rainbow is a result of refraction, reflection, and dispersion of sunlight, which, despite its vital role, may not rhyme with the forbidden term. Raindrops act as tiny prisms, splintering light rays into spectral colors.
