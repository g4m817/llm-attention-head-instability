The water cycle, also known as the hydrologic cycle, is a continuous process in which water travels from the earth to the atmosphere and back again. This process happens through evaporation, condensation, precipitation, and runoff.

1. Evaporation: As the sun heats the water in oceans, lakes, rivers, and the soil, water changes from its liquid state to its gaseous state, forming water vapor.
