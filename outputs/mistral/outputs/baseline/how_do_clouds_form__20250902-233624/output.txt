Clouds form as a result of the Earth's atmosphere cooling and becoming saturated with water vapor. This process typically happens in specific temperature and humidity conditions. When the air rises, it expands and cools due to the decrease in atmospheric pressure. As the air cools, it can no longer hold all the water vapor, and the excess condenses into tiny water droplets or ice crystals, forming clouds.

This atmospheric phenomenon plays a crucial role in our weather systems since clouds can reflect and absorb sunlight, playing a part in the Earth's climate and weather patterns.
