In the context of the legal system, the role of the Supreme Court can be described as the highest court in the judicial hierarchy of a country. It serves as the final interpreter of the law, resolving disputes that involve the federal government, states, and private individuals. The Supreme Court ensures that laws and government actions are consistent with the constitution and other fundamental laws of the land. It also has the power to declare laws unconstitutional if they violate the rights and freedoms guaranteed by the constitution. However, it's important to note that the specific roles and powers of the Supreme Court may vary depending on the country's legal system.
