In the United States, the process of a bill becoming a law is a multi-step process known as legislative process. Here's a simplified version of the steps:

1. Introduction: A bill is introduced in either the House of Representatives or the Senate by a member of Congress.

2. Committee Assignment: The bill is then assigned to a committee for review and discussion.

3. Committee Consideration: The committee may hold hearings, invite experts to testify, and debate the bill. If the committee approves the bill, it is sent to the floor for further consideration.
