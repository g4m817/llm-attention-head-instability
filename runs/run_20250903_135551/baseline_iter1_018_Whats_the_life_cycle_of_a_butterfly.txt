The life cycle of a butterfly can be described as follows:

1. Egg: The life of a butterfly begins when a female butterfly lays eggs on a suitable food plant for the caterpillars.

2. Larva (Caterpillar): After hatching, the caterpillar feeds on the plant, growing and shedding its skin several times.

3. Pupa (Chrysalis): Once it has grown enough, the caterpillar attaches itself to a surface and forms a chrysalis, or cocoon, around itself. During this stage, the caterpillar undergoes a transformation called metamorphosis.
