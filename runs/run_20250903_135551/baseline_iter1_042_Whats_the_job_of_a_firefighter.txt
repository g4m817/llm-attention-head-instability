The job of a firefighter involves responding to fires and emergencies, extinguishing fires, rescuing people and animals, and providing emergency medical services. They work to protect lives, property, and the environment from the effects of fire, as well as other hazards such as chemical spills and natural disasters. They are trained to work in a variety of environments, including buildings, wilderness areas, and waterways. They may also be responsible for conducting fire safety inspections and educating the public about fire safety. They are often part of a team and work in shifts, as fires can occur at any time. They are essential public safety workers who put their lives on the line to protect others.
