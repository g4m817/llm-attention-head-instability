A bill must go through several stages to become a law in the United States. First, it is introduced to Congress, either the House of Representatives or the Senate. It is then assigned a number and sent to a committee for consideration. If the committee approves the bill, it is reported to the floor of the chamber where it was introduced for further discussion and debate. After passing a vote, the bill is sent to the other chamber, where it must go through the same process. If the second chamber also passes the bill, it is sent to the President for final approval.
